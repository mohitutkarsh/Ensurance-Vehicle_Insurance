15)What is the difference between a Third-party Liability Insurance and a Comprehensive two/four wheeler Insurance policy?

A Third-party Liability Insurance is mandatory as per The Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. It is designed to help third parties that might be affected by your two/four wheeler in case of an accident. On the other hand, a Comprehensive two/four wheeler policy includes the mandatory Third-party Liability insurance and offers wide insurance coverage for your vehicle. With a Comprehensive policy, your vehicle will be insured against risks such as accidents, theft, calamities, etc.

16)How do insurance companies determine the two/four wheeler insurance premium?

Insurance companies primarily determine two/four wheeler insurance premium based on the make, model, age, and registration location of your two/four wheeler. Your two/four wheeler insurance premium is also dependent on the type of policy chosen and your claims history.

17)What is the meaning of Depreciation in two/four wheeler insurance?

Depreciation means the reduction in the value of an asset due to wear and tear over a period of time. With regards to two/four wheeler insurance, this value is calculated as per the depreciation rates provided by the Insurance Regulatory And Development Authority of India (IRDAI).

18)What is the meaning of IDV?

IDV means Insured Declared Value. It is the value you will receive in case your insured two/four wheeler suffers total damage or is stolen. It is not your two/four wheeler’s resale value.

19)What is the meaning of Policy Period?

Policy period means the duration for which your insurance policy is active. Generally, in case of two/four wheeler, this period is of one year. Make sure to renew your two/four wheeler insurance before the expiry of the policy period.

20)What is the meaning of No Claim Bonus?

No Claim Bonus is a discount given to you by the insurance company while renewing your insurance. It is kind of a reward for not raising a claim during your policy period.

21)What if my policy is misplaced?

22)How will the depreciation of vehicle parts be calculated?

Depreciation will be calculated as per following: ->Rubber, Nylon, Plastic Parts, Tyres and Tubes, Batteries, and Air Bags : 50% depreciation ->Fiberglass components : 30% depreciation ->All parts made of glass : 0 depreciation ->Material cost of the paint in case of a paint job : 50% depreciation Note: If a consolidated amount is charged for the job, then 25% of the total cost will be considered as the material cost.

->6 months or less : 0% depreciation ->Between 6 months and 1 year : 5 % depreciation ->Between 1 and 2 years : 10 % depreciation ->Between 2 and 3 years : 15 % depreciation ->Between 3 and 4 years : 25 % depreciation ->Between 4 and 5 years : 35 % depreciation ->Between 5 and 10 years : 40 % depreciation ->More than 10 years : 50 % depreciation

25)Which is the best type of two/four wheeler insurance?

One can choose to buy either of the following types of two/four wheeler insurance policies: ->Third-party Liability ->Comprehensive insurance A third-party liability policy will offer protection only against third-party damage. 26)What does ‘cashless' mean in Cashless Garage?

To make the claims process faster, insurance companies have tie-ups with local garages. Such garages form a network. If you get your two/four wheeler repaired from a network garage, you can avail cashless repairs. In this process, you do not need to worry about collecting physical bills and then submitting the paperwork for raising a claim. Your insurer coordinates with the garage making the process hassle-free for you. It is not a 100% cashless claim, you need to bear a minimal cost under deductibles.
